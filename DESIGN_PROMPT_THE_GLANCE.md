## What this is for

The Healing Model organizes each trip's photos on its own. Rarely — measured: about five
questions across four whole trips, then never again — it genuinely needs a human glance.
That glance must clear Jonathan's bar: **as enjoyable as everything else on a trip, or
don't ask.** Not a chore, not a form, not a CAPTCHA-farm. Reminiscing with a payoff.

## What the engine now guarantees (design against these, they are real)

- **Rare and consolidated.** One question per MOMENT, never per photo. Questions below an
  answerability floor are never asked at all — they stay softly placed instead of nagging.
- **Human-shaped content.** Each question arrives as: a moment name from the photos
  themselves ("Fourth of July Parade", "Playing Connect Four"), 2–3 NAMED candidate
  places (with their kind — beach / restaurant / museum), the member photos for thumbs,
  and two honest numbers: REACH (how many photos this answer settles) and TAUGHT (how
  many lookalike photos elsewhere it teaches).
- **Recognition, not recall.** Questions are chosen so the answer is visible in the
  photos (candidates of different KINDS rank first). The family looks and knows; they
  never research.
- **Every answer cascades.** Measured: one answer settled 13 photos; five emptied the
  whole queue. An answer also teaches the corpus (lookalike exemplars) — it reaches other
  trips.

## The design problem (the fun)

1. **The reward is the ripple — make it VISIBLE.** The deep mechanic: you tap once and
   watch the afternoon assemble itself. "That settled 12 more photos" isn't copy, it's
   the payoff moment. Design the ripple: how does the family SEE the photos snap into
   place? (Animation on the card? The album visibly tidying behind it? The settled gold
   line growing?) This is the game-feel WITHOUT formal game mechanics — no points, no
   streaks, no badges (settled: the family flees admin and gamification alike).
2. **Two modes, one voice.** The rare ASK (this prompt) and the everyday SHOW
   (§4 of the plan: firm core / soft edges, the graded read shown calmly, Carrot-style
   honesty in our warm idiom). They should feel like one personality: the app thinking
   aloud about the trip, occasionally wondering something the family would enjoy
   answering.
3. **The stack, not the drip.** When a trip has 3–5 questions, is the delightful shape
   one-a-day (the S1 budget) or a tiny "help me finish the album" flip-through session
   the family chooses to enter (photo up, two big names, tap, next — 30 seconds total)?
   Design both; recommend one. The budget rule stays: never interrupt, never nag,
   silent-empty when there's nothing worth asking.
4. **Wit, per lens.** The existing per-person voices apply (Aurelia's lowercase, Rafa's
   energy, warm plain language for the adults). The question is a moment of shared
   remembering — "which lunch was the Connect Four game?" — not a data request. Copy
   must never over-promise (the S1 honesty lessons hold: a pick only claims what it
   actually files).

## Constraints (non-negotiable, all already load-bearing)

- Masking/visibility rules per viewer (surprises never leak through a question).
- The provenance chain: an answer writes source:'confirmed' through the S1 path — locks,
  re-heal, Level-2 coord propagation all already exist. The design spends NO new writes.
- a11y to the S1 bar (axe-clean, focus order, hit targets).
- Silent empty: no reserved space when there is no worthy question.
- PHOTO_CONFIRM_MODE stays off until the flip gate; design ships inert.

## The question PALETTE (kinds — Jonathan's three were examples, not the space)

Organized by what the answer DOES to the machine. Every kind competes under the SAME
worthAsking floor and delight budget — more kinds must never mean more questions:

1. **Picking** (built): resolve a known conflict — "parade route, or the off-route stop?"
2. **Expanding the world**: the closed-world escape — "somewhere else?" as a first-class
   peer of the candidates (never a buried free-text), and CHRISTENING — "you were clearly
   somewhere for these ten — what do you call it?" (the answer creates a place that exists
   forever after).
3. **Structure**: the day's shape, not a photo's place — "were you all together, or split
   up?" (vivid, near-perfectly answerable, restructures the whole day), one-moment-or-two,
   and ordering ("parade first, or beach first?") for time-stripped photos.
4. **Calibrating the rules** (the biggest cascade): a pattern, not a photo — "Helen's
   photos land a day late — right?" One tap re-weights a channel across EVERY trip.
5. **Gifts**: the answer IS content — "what was happening here?" → a caption/story that
   enriches the album (the Record's third tense), and as a side effect manufactures the
   caption corpus D15 was parked for. Reminiscence first, extraction incidental.
6. **Spot-checks**: retire risk before a big cascade propagates — "quick check: cabin
   week, right?"

Cross-cutting: WHO (route to the likely knower — the uploader; Rafa's question can be a
game for Rafa) and WHEN (answerability decays — fresh beats archaeology; mid-trip
attention is sacred, never interrupted; and some answers are never asked for at all —
lingering/sharing behavior is an implicit answer).
