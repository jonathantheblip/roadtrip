## TOPIC 2: AXIOM SPACE HOUSTON

### What Axiom Space does

Axiom Space is building the **world's first commercial space station** — the planned successor to the International Space Station. Founded in 2016 by Michael Suffredini (NASA's former ISS Program Manager) and Kam Ghaffarian, the company operates across three pillars: **private astronaut missions** to the ISS using SpaceX Crew Dragon, **commercial space station modules** (the first module targets 2027 launch — Axiom is the only company with NASA permission to attach commercial modules to the ISS), and **next-generation spacesuits** (the AxEMU, designed with Prada for Artemis lunar missions). The company has **500–1,000 employees**, is valued over $1 billion, and employs former astronauts Peggy Whitson, Michael López-Alegría, and Koichi Wakata. Their most recent mission, **Ax-4** (June–July 2025), was an 18-day mission carrying the first-ever ISS astronauts from India, Poland, and Hungary, conducting 60+ experiments from 31 nations.

### Location

Axiom has two Houston facilities, both in the Clear Lake / NASA area:

- **Headquarters & Mission Control:** 1290 Hercules Ave, Houston, TX 77058 — corporate offices plus their Mission Control Center (MCC-A), which is the 12th Ground Segment Partner for the ISS Program. Mon–Fri 8am–5pm.
- **Houston Spaceport / Ellington Campus:** 2539 Enterprise Shuttle St, Houston, TX 77034 — a 22-acre campus with a 106,000+ sq ft Assembly, Integration & Testing facility where space station modules are physically being built. High-bay production, fabrication, welding, and machining operations.

Both are **minutes from NASA's Johnson Space Center** and Space Center Houston (the public visitor center). Same neighborhood.

### Chris's tour — what to expect

**Axiom does not offer public tours.** There is no visitor center, no ticketed experience, no "Visit Us" page. This is an active private aerospace facility with proprietary hardware and ITAR (arms regulation) restrictions.

What Chris is offering is an **employee-hosted private tour**, which is the standard way non-employees see inside companies like Axiom. Here's what the family should expect:

- **Chris will pre-register them as visitors** with security/facilities. Adults will need government-issued photo ID at the front desk for temporary visitor badges.
- **Visitors must be escorted at all times** by Chris. Photography will likely be restricted in certain areas — Chris will know the specifics.
- **What they'll likely see:** The Mission Control Center (real consoles with live ISS data — not a museum replica), spacesuit development areas (AxEMU prototypes for Artemis moon missions), and if at the Spaceport facility, the high-bay production area where actual space station modules are being assembled. The specifics depend on which facility Chris works at and his access level.
- **Kids are almost certainly welcome** with advance arrangement. Chris should confirm with his security team whether there's a minimum age, and whether the kids need any documentation.
- **Duration:** Likely **60–90 minutes** for a family visit.

### Kid-friendliness

**Aurelia (13): Extremely engaging.** A 13-year-old fully grasps the concept of building a space station and moon suits. Seeing a real Mission Control Center — not a museum replica — is mind-blowing at this age. The personal connection to Uncle Chris makes it even more powerful. This could genuinely be a formative experience.

**Rafa (almost 5): Moderately engaging.** He won't understand the technical details, but he'll respond to "Uncle Chris works where they build SPACESHIPS," big rooms with big hardware and screens, and any spacesuits or helmets he can see. **Plan for 45–60 minutes max** before restlessness. The high-bay at the Spaceport facility is visually impressive — giant warehouse-like spaces with spacecraft hardware.

**Recommended approach:** Do the Axiom tour in the morning when kids are freshest. Then drive minutes to **Space Center Houston** (the NASA public visitor center) for the interactive, hands-on exhibits designed for all ages. The Axiom visit makes the Space Center experience even more meaningful: "We just saw where they're BUILDING the next space station."

## TOPIC 3: RICE UNIVERSITY CAMPUS

### Getting there from 1301 Marshall St

Rice University (6100 Main St) is **2–3 miles south** of the Menil campus — about a **10-minute drive** in normal traffic. Not realistically walkable with a 5-year-old, but a quick trip. The two campuses bookend the Museum District, with Hermann Park, the MFAH, and the Contemporary Arts Museum between them.

### The campus walk

Rice's **300-acre campus** is one of the most beautiful in America — **4,300+ trees**, 100+ public artworks, and a unified Mediterranean Revival architectural vocabulary designed by Ralph Adams Cram. The campus is open to public visitors. **You can drive onto campus** to reach parking lots, but the interior is largely pedestrian. The **outer loop** (a popular 2.92-mile crushed gravel path around the perimeter) takes about an hour to walk; a focused tour of highlights takes **60–90 minutes**.

**Visitor parking:** The **Greenbriar Lot** (corner of Greenbriar & University, Entrances 13A/13B) is the best value at **$4/day flat rate**, credit card only. Central Campus Garage is more central at $1/10 min, $13 daily max.

### Must-see landmarks

**Lovett Hall (1912)** is Rice's iconic first building — rose-hued brick, patterned marble, colored tiles, Texas pink granite, designed by Ralph Adams Cram in Mediterranean Revival style. Enter through the **Sallyport**, the grand archway through which students enter at matriculation and exit at commencement. Jonathan walked through it; Helen will appreciate the architecture.

The **Academic Quadrangle** between Lovett Hall and Fondren Library was **completely redesigned in 2024** by Nelson Byrd Woltz Landscape Architects — three zones (ceremonial, daily life, community) with curving paths flanked by Monterrey oaks and 24,000+ native plants in Rice's school colors. The Founder's Memorial statue of William Marsh Rice has been relocated to a limestone terrace with contextual interpretation acknowledging his history as a slaveowner.

Other highlights: **Herzstein Hall** has acoustic whispering niches (Rafa will love this — stand on opposite sides of the main doors and whisper to each other). **Rice Stadium** is where JFK delivered the "We choose to go to the Moon" speech in 1962. **Moody Center for the Arts** has free exhibitions (Tue–Sat 10am–5pm). Michael Heizer's sculptures on the Engineering Quad. The **Rice Public Art App** enables a self-guided tour of 100+ works.

### ⚠️ Turrell Skyspace — currently closed

**The James Turrell "Twilight Epiphany" Skyspace is closed** due to adjacent construction on the Jones Graduate School of Business expansion. It closed May 2024. The official Moody Center page now says **"summer 2026"** for reopening — so it will **not be open** during the April 23–24 visit. The closure has enabled an upgrade to the original 2012 LED lighting system with a modern system approved by Turrell himself. The exterior structure may be visible from a distance but the light sequences are not running and the installation is not accessible.

**When it does reopen:** Free, open daily 6am–10pm. LED light sequences at sunrise (begins 40 min before) and sunset (begins ~10 min before), each lasting ~40 minutes. No reservations. Seats 120.

**Alternative Turrell in Houston:** The Museum of Fine Arts, Houston has James Turrell's **"The Light Inside"** — a luminous tunnel installation. Free with museum admission on Thursdays.

### Food and coffee near campus

**On campus:** Brochstein Pavilion (modern glass student center, coffee and pastries) and Rice Coffeehouse (student-run, affordable). **Rice Village** is a 5–10 minute walk west — Houston's oldest shopping district with 350+ restaurants and shops. Best bets: **Badolina Bakery & Cafe** (Israeli-style, great pastries and shaded patio), **Bluestone Lane** (Australian-inspired coffee and brunch), **Hungry's Cafe** (Mediterranean, popular terrace on Rice Blvd — good vegetarian options).

## TOPIC 4: TIKTOK DEEP LINK FORMAT

### The problem

`https://www.tiktok.com/search?q=QUERY` opens to a blank screen when launched from a PWA in standalone mode on iOS. This happens because iOS PWAs use a lightweight WebKit web view that **does not trigger Universal Link interception** the way Safari does. The URL loads inside the PWA's in-app browser rather than handing off to the TikTok app.

### Available TikTok URL schemes

| Scheme | Path | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `tiktok://` | `video/{video_id}`, `user/profile/@username`, `music/{music_id}`, `hashtag/{name}`, `feed` | Modern simplified scheme, works iOS + Android |
| `snssdk1233://` | `aweme/detail/{id}`, `user/profile/{uid}`, `feed?refer=web` | Internal TikTok international scheme |
| `snssdk1128://` | `search`, `search/trending` | **Douyin (Chinese TikTok) only** — not confirmed for international |

### Critical finding: no confirmed search deep link exists

**There is no publicly documented deep link scheme for opening TikTok international's search with a pre-filled query.** The `snssdk1128://search` path is confirmed only for Douyin (Chinese version). Neither `tiktok://search?q=QUERY` nor `snssdk1233://search?q=QUERY` is documented in the community-maintained TikTok URL scheme repository (330 GitHub stars). TikTok's official developer documentation covers advertising deep links but does not expose a search endpoint.

### Best approach: open in Safari as intermediary

From a PWA on iOS, the most reliable pattern is to force the URL to open in Safari (the system browser) rather than the PWA's internal web view. Safari properly triggers Universal Link interception, which can hand off to the TikTok app.

```javascript
// Best approach: force open in Safari, which triggers Universal Links
function openTikTokSearch(query) {
  const url = `https://www.tiktok.com/search?q=${encodeURIComponent(query)}`;
  window.open(url, '_blank');  // Opens in Safari from PWA standalone mode
}
```

`window.open(url, '_blank')` from a standalone PWA on iOS opens Safari, and Safari's Universal Link handling should then redirect to the TikTok app if installed. If TikTok is not installed, the user lands on the TikTok web search results page — a natural fallback.

### Alternative: custom scheme with fallback

```javascript
function openTikTokSearch(query) {
  const webUrl = `https://www.tiktok.com/search?q=${encodeURIComponent(query)}`;
  
  // Try custom scheme (may show confirmation dialog on iOS)
  const schemeUrl = `tiktok://search?q=${encodeURIComponent(query)}`;
  
  // Attempt scheme via hidden iframe
  const iframe = document.createElement('iframe');
  iframe.style.display = 'none';
  iframe.src = schemeUrl;
  document.body.appendChild(iframe);
  
  // Fallback to web after 1.5s if app doesn't open
  setTimeout(() => {
    document.body.removeChild(iframe);
    window.open(webUrl, '_blank');
  }, 1500);
}
```

**Caveat:** The `tiktok://search?q=` path is **undocumented and unconfirmed** — it may or may not work depending on TikTok's app version. The iframe approach can also trigger iOS security dialogs. The `window.open` + Safari approach is more reliable.

### Third-party deep linking services

Services like **URLgenius**, **Linkrunner**, and **JotURL** handle device detection, scheme routing, and fallback automatically. They generate smart URLs that detect whether TikTok is installed, serve the correct scheme, and fall back to web or App Store. This adds a dependency but eliminates edge-case debugging. Worth considering if the PWA serves many users.

### Summary recommendation

Use `window.open('https://www.tiktok.com/search?q=' + encodeURIComponent(query), '_blank')` as the primary approach. This opens Safari, which properly handles Universal Links to TikTok. If TikTok isn't installed, the user sees web search results — a clean fallback. The blank-screen problem is specifically caused by the PWA's internal WebKit view failing to trigger Universal Link interception, and `_blank` bypasses that.

## TOPIC 5: POINTS OF INTEREST BY STATE

### Connecticut (I-84 corridor)

**1. Wadsworth Atheneum Museum of Art**
600 Main Street, Hartford, CT 06103
**For Helen** · Type: art
America's oldest continuously operating public art museum holds nearly 50,000 works spanning 5,000 years — Caravaggio, Monet, Dalí — in five connected buildings ranging from Gothic Revival to Brutalist. Free for children under 12. Thu–Fri noon–5pm, Sat–Sun 10am–5pm.

**2. Yale University Art Gallery**
1111 Chapel Street, New Haven, CT 06510
**For Helen** · Type: art
The oldest university art museum in the Western Hemisphere houses 300,000+ objects from ancient to contemporary in Louis Kahn's landmark 1953 building. Always free admission — the kind of collection that rivals major city museums without the crowds.

**3. Weir Farm National Historical Park**
735 Nod Hill Road, Wilton, CT 06897
**For Helen** · Type: art / nature
The only National Park dedicated to American painting — J. Alden Weir's 60-acre Impressionist landscape where you can still paint en plein air through their free "Take Part in Art" program. Studios, gardens, and Weir Pond feel like walking into a canvas. Free, open daily dawn to dusk.

**4. Gillette Castle State Park**
67 River Road, East Haddam, CT 06423
**For Aurelia** · Type: photo / architecture
An eccentric medieval-style castle built by the actor who played Sherlock Holmes, perched on a hilltop overlooking the Connecticut River — it looks like it belongs in a fantasy film. The 184 acres of trails and river views are extremely photogenic.

**5. Mark Twain House & Museum**
351 Farmington Avenue, Hartford, CT 06105
**For both** · Type: architecture
Samuel Clemens's Victorian Gothic mansion with Tiffany-designed interiors where he wrote *Huckleberry Finn* and *Tom Sawyer*. The ornate rooms are peak Gilded Age aesthetic.

**6. Mystic Seaport Museum**
75 Greenmanville Avenue, Mystic, CT 06355
**For Aurelia** · Type: photo
A 19th-century coastal village re-created on 19 acres with the last surviving wooden whaling ship and a working shipyard. The maritime aesthetic and historic village buildings photograph beautifully.

### New York (Hudson Valley / Catskills)

**1. Dia Beacon**
3 Beekman Street, Beacon, NY 12508
**For Helen** · Type: art
Nearly 300,000 square feet of contemporary art in a repurposed 1929 Nabisco factory — site-specific installations by Richard Serra, Dan Flavin, Donald Judd, Agnes Martin, Louise Bourgeois. Natural skylights flood the vast galleries. A new 8-acre South Garden opens in 2026. Fri–Mon 10am–5pm; $25 adults, under-12 free.

**2. Storm King Art Center**
20 Old Pleasant Hill Road, New Windsor, NY 12553
**For both** · Type: art / nature
Five hundred acres of monumental sculpture amid rolling Hudson Valley hills — Calder, di Suvero, Henry Moore, Maya Lin, and Andy Goldsworthy's 2,728-foot earthwork *Storm King Wall*. This is the kind of place where art and landscape become the same thing. April–November, timed tickets.

**3. Olana State Historic Site**
5720 State Route 9G, Hudson, NY 12534
**For Helen** · Type: architecture / nature
Hudson River School painter Frederic Edwin Church designed this Persian-inspired villa and its 250-acre landscape as a total artwork — panoramic views of the Hudson Valley and Catskill Mountains from five miles of carriage roads. 2026 marks Church's 200th birthday with special celebrations. Landscape free; house tours $12–$22 Fri–Sun.

**4. Walkway Over the Hudson**
61 Parker Avenue, Poughkeepsie, NY 12601
**For Aurelia** · Type: photo
The world's longest elevated pedestrian bridge — 1.28 miles across the Hudson River, 212 feet above the water. The views in every direction are stunning and extremely shareable. Free, open daily 7am–sunset.

**5. Beacon Main Street**
Main Street, Beacon, NY 12508
**For Aurelia** · Type: shopping / food
A charming arts-hub downtown with dozens of galleries, vintage shops, and trendy cafes — the perfect companion stop to Dia Beacon. The whole street has an effortlessly cool small-town aesthetic that feels curated without trying.

**6. Thomas Cole National Historic Site**
218 Spring Street, Catskill, NY 12414
**For Helen** · Type: art / architecture
Home and studio of the founder of the Hudson River School, connected to Olana via the Hudson River Skywalk pedestrian bridge. Walking between these two painters' homes across the Hudson is a pilgrimage for anyone who loves American landscape art.

**7. Blue Ridge Parkway — Northern Terminus**
Rockfish Gap (I-64/US 250), Waynesboro, VA
**For Helen** · Type: nature
"America's Favorite Drive" continues south from Skyline Drive. The northern miles feature Humpback Rocks pioneer farmstead with sweeping panoramic views and wildflower meadows. Free, no entrance fee.

### Tennessee (Elizabethton + Chattanooga + Nashville)

**1. Elizabethton Covered Bridge**
E Elk Ave at Hattie Ave, Elizabethton, TN 37643
**For Aurelia** · Type: photo
An 1882 covered bridge spanning 134 feet across the Doe River — white-painted clapboard in a riverside park. Summer Saturday evenings bring "Covered Bridge Jams" concerts. Best photographed at sunset, when the light through the bridge is gorgeous.

**2. Roan Mountain Rhododendron Gardens**
Roan Mountain State Park, TN 37687 (~30 min from Elizabethton)
**For Helen** · Type: nature
The world's largest natural rhododendron garden — in mid-to-late June, the mountaintop erupts in a sea of purple and magenta. Even outside bloom, the Carvers Gap section offers one of the most scenic stretches of the Appalachian Trail with grassy balds and 360-degree views.

**3. Lookout Mountain / Ruby Falls**
1720 Scenic Highway, Chattanooga, TN 37409
**For Aurelia** · Type: nature / photo

**4. Hunter Museum of American Art**
10 Bluff View, Chattanooga, TN 37403
**For Helen** · Type: art / architecture
American art from the Colonial period through contemporary, housed in a 1904 mansion connected to a dramatic modern glass addition cantilevered over the Tennessee River bluffs. The building itself — old mansion meets glass cliff — is as compelling as the collection.

**5. Nashville Murals**
Various locations — concentrate on 12 South, the Gulch, and East Nashville
**For Aurelia** · Type: photo
Nashville has become a mural capital — the "Wings" mural (the Gulch), "I Believe in Nashville" (12 South), and dozens of colorful walls throughout East Nashville. The concentration in 12 South pairs perfectly with boutique shopping and trendy cafes.

**6. Draper James — 12 South Nashville**
2608 12th Ave S, Nashville, TN 37204
**For Aurelia** · Type: shopping
Reese Witherspoon's Southern lifestyle brand flagship — preppy, colorful, and extremely Instagrammable inside and out. The whole 12 South strip (White's Mercantile, Imogene + Willie, Frothy Monkey) is a curated browse.

### Alabama (Birmingham area)

**1. Birmingham Museum of Art**
2000 Rev Abraham Woods Jr Blvd, Birmingham, AL 35203
**For Helen** · Type: art
Over 27,000 works spanning ancient to contemporary — one of the finest art museums in the Southeast, and it's **always free**. The Asian, European, American, and African collections rival museums in much larger cities.

**2. Birmingham Botanical Gardens**
2612 Lane Park Rd, Birmingham, AL 35223
**For Helen** · Type: nature
Sixty-seven acres with 30+ themed gardens including a Japanese garden, rose garden, and tropical conservatory. Free admission year-round. The kind of place Helen could spend a whole morning without running out of things to notice.

**3. Vulcan Park and Museum**
1701 Valley View Dr, Birmingham, AL 35209
**For Aurelia** · Type: photo
The world's largest cast iron statue — 56 feet tall on a 124-foot pedestal, the Roman god Vulcan overlooking Birmingham. Panoramic views from the observation deck. Created for the 1904 World's Fair. Note: the elevator has been intermittently out of service — stairs are available.

**4. Sloss Furnaces National Historic Landmark**
20 32nd Street North, Birmingham, AL 35222
**For both** · Type: architecture / photo
A preserved blast furnace complex that looks like an industrial cathedral. The rusted steel infrastructure against the sky is raw and dramatic — unlike anything else on the trip. Free admission, Tue–Sat.

**5. Pepper Place Saturday Market**
2829 2nd Ave S, Birmingham, AL 35233
**For both** · Type: food / shopping
Birmingham's vibrant Saturday farmers' market (April–December) in the Pepper Place design district. Local produce, baked goods, artisanal coffee, prepared foods. Great vegetarian grazing. The surrounding neighborhood has murals and galleries worth exploring.

**6. Railroad Park**
1600 1st Ave S, Birmingham, AL 35233
**For Aurelia** · Type: photo / nature
A 19-acre urban park in downtown Birmingham with walking trails, a lake, and a playground (for Rafa). Hosts concerts, outdoor movies, and festivals. A good stretch-your-legs stop with the city skyline as backdrop.

### Mississippi (Jackson / Vicksburg / McComb corridor)

**1. Mississippi Arts + Entertainment Experience (The MAX)**
2155 Front Street, Meridian, MS 39301
**For both** · Type: art / music
A modern, immersive museum celebrating Mississippi's extraordinary contributions to arts and entertainment — blues, rock, literature, film. Interactive exhibits bring the stories to life. Highly rated and engaging for teens.

**2. Natchez Historic Antebellum Homes**
Various addresses — start at Natchez Visitor Center, 640 S Canal St, Natchez, MS 39120
**For Helen** · Type: architecture
Over 1,000 structures on the National Register and 13 National Historic Landmarks. Key homes open year-round: Rosalie Mansion, Stanton Hall (Greek Revival that inspired Disney's Haunted Mansion), and Longwood (the largest octagonal house in America, unfinished since the Civil War). Spring Pilgrimage (March–April) opens 20+ private homes.

**3. Ground Zero Blues Club**
387 Delta Ave, Clarksdale, MS 38614
**For Aurelia** · Type: music / food
Co-owned by Morgan Freeman in a repurposed warehouse with juke-joint styling — mismatched chairs, Christmas lights, graffiti walls. Authentic Delta blues performances Wed–Sat with Southern food (hot tamales, fried green tomatoes). The raw, real atmosphere is the opposite of polished and that's what makes it cool.

**4. Natchez Under-the-Hill**
Silver Street, Natchez, MS 39120
**For both** · Type: photo / food
A historic riverfront district below the bluffs where cotton once loaded onto steamboats. Mississippi River sunsets from the front porch rocking chairs of the Under-the-Hill Saloon are spectacular. Live music on weekends.

**5. Vicksburg National Military Park**
3201 Clay Street, Vicksburg, MS 39183
**For both** · Type: nature / photo
A 16-mile driving tour through the rolling terrain of the pivotal 1863 siege, with 1,300+ monuments and the USS Cairo gunboat museum. The landscape is beautiful and the scale of the memorial field is genuinely moving.

**6. Delta Blues Museum**
1 Blues Alley, Clarksdale, MS 38614
**For Aurelia** · Type: music
Dedicated to preserving Delta blues history in the renovated Clarksdale freight depot. Artifacts from Muddy Waters, Robert Johnson, B.B. King. Pair with Ground Zero next door for a full Clarksdale blues stop.

**1. Historic Downtown Ruston**

**For both** · Type: nature / food
Louisiana's largest peach orchard growing the famous "Ruston Red." Seasonal u-pick (June–July), farm store, peach ice cream. Even outside picking season, the rolling orchard rows are picturesque.

### Texas (I-20 East Texas + DFW + I-45 + Houston)

**1. Kimbell Art Museum**
3333 Camp Bowie Blvd, Fort Worth, TX 76107
**For Helen** · Type: art / architecture
Louis Kahn's 1972 masterpiece — widely considered one of the most perfect museum buildings ever designed. The cycloid vaults and natural light system are breathtaking. The collection is small but exceptional: Caravaggio, Michelangelo, Monet, Cézanne. Renzo Piano's 2013 expansion pavilion across the lawn is equally stunning. **Free permanent collection.** Just blocks from Don Artemio.

**2. Modern Art Museum of Fort Worth**
3200 Darnell St, Fort Worth, TX 76107
**For Helen** · Type: art / architecture
Tadao Ando's concrete-and-glass building reflected in a serene water garden — one of the most architecturally significant modern art museums in America. The collection spans post-WWII to contemporary. Together with the Kimbell and the Amon Carter next door, Fort Worth's Cultural District is an extraordinary cluster. **Free on Sundays.**

**3. Fort Worth Stockyards**
131 E Exchange Ave, Fort Worth, TX 76164
**For Aurelia** · Type: photo / food / shopping
The daily cattle drive down Exchange Avenue (11:30am and 4pm) is a spectacle — real Texas longhorns driven by real cowboys through a National Historic District. The whole Stockyards area has been revitalized with shops, restaurants, and Billy Bob's Texas (the world's largest honky-tonk). Aurelia will find the cowboy-meets-aesthetic vibe genuinely unique.

**4. Buc-ee's**
Multiple locations on I-45 and I-35
**For Aurelia** · Type: food / shopping
The Texas mega-gas-station phenomenon — spotlessly clean, 60,000+ square feet, beaver nuggets, brisket sandwiches, walls of candy, and the cleanest restrooms in America. It's a cultural experience. Rafa will lose his mind. The one in Madisonville (I-45) or New Braunfels (I-35) are among the largest.

**5. The Menil Collection + Rothko Chapel**
1533 Sul Ross St / 3900 Yupon St, Houston, TX 77006
**For Helen** · Type: art
They're staying steps away, so this deserves a proper pitch: Renzo Piano's luminous 1987 building houses 17,000+ works (ancient to contemporary) on a 30-acre campus with the Cy Twombly Gallery, Dan Flavin installation, and Drawing Institute. Across the lawn, the Rothko Chapel is a non-denominational sanctuary with 14 Mark Rothko paintings — the most profoundly quiet space on the trip. Barnett Newman's *Broken Obelisk* stands in the reflecting pool outside. **Always free.**

**6. Houston Montrose street art + shopping**
Westheimer Road and surrounding streets, Houston, TX 77006
**For Aurelia** · Type: photo / shopping
Houston's most eclectic neighborhood — the Biscuit Paint Wall (rainbow drip mural, #BiscuitPaintWall on Instagram), vintage shops (Pavement, Leopard Lounge), Montrose Collective (Mejuri, Reformation, Sophie's cocktail bar), and colorful storefronts. The whole neighborhood rewards wandering with a camera.

**7. Space Center Houston**
1601 E NASA Pkwy, Houston, TX 77058
**For both** · Type: photo / nature
The public visitor center for NASA's Johnson Space Center — tram tours to Mission Control and astronaut training facilities, the Saturn V rocket, space shuttle replica Independence, and interactive exhibits. Pair with the Axiom visit for the ultimate space day. Rafa gets hands-on exhibits; Aurelia gets iconic photo spots with actual spacecraft.

**8. Canton First Monday Trade Days**
800 First Monday Lane, Canton, TX 75103
**For Aurelia** · Type: shopping
The oldest, largest, and most famous flea market in the U.S. — over 5,000 vendors across hundreds of acres selling vintage, handmade, antique, and unique finds. Runs the Thursday–Sunday before the first Monday of each month. If the timing aligns (they'd need to check the April 2026 dates), it's a massive treasure hunt.
