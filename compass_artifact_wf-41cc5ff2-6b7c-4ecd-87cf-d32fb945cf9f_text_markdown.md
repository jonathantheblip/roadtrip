# Road trip energy-burn stops: Belmont MA to Houston TX

A 4-year-old can't sit still for more than 90 minutes — and across five driving days covering **2,500+ miles**, this family needs **20–25 verified stops** where a preschooler can run, climb, splash, and burn energy regardless of weather. Below is every stop organized by driving segment, with full addresses, indoor and outdoor options at each interval, and the specific details that matter when you're pulling off a highway with a squirming kid in the backseat.

---

## Day 1: Belmont MA to Catskills NY — Friday, April 17

**Route:** I-90 W → I-84 W through Connecticut → NY-17/Taconic toward Catskills | **~4 hours driving**

### Interval 1 — Hartford CT (~90 minutes from Belmont)

**OUTDOOR: Bushnell Park — Carousel and Playground**
- **Address:** 1 Jewell St, Hartford, CT 06103
- **Type:** Outdoor
- **What's there:** America's second-oldest public park — **50 acres** with a children's playground and a stunning 1914 hand-carved carousel with 48 horses, stained-glass panels, and a Wurlitzer organ. Paths, pond, green space for running.
- **Detour:** Right off I-84, under 5 minutes
- **Cost:** Park free; carousel rides **$2** each
- **Hours:** Park open daily 7 AM–8 PM. Carousel runs weekends 12–5 PM (seasonal — verify at bushnellpark.org for April Friday hours)
- **Bathrooms:** Yes
- **Fenced:** No, but the park is set back from roads

**INDOOR: Connecticut Science Center**
- **Address:** 250 Columbus Blvd, Hartford, CT 06103
- **Type:** Indoor
- **What's there:** 150+ hands-on exhibits across 10 galleries, including dedicated early-childhood areas. A 4-year-old can easily spend 30–45 minutes in the kids' zone without needing to see the whole museum.
- **Detour:** 5 minutes from I-84
- **Cost:** ~$28/adult, ~$20/child (ages 3–17)
- **Hours:** Tue–Sun 10 AM–5 PM
- **Bathrooms:** Yes
- **Age restrictions:** None; toddler-specific exhibits available

### Interval 2 — Waterbury CT (~45 minutes from Hartford)

**OUTDOOR: Fulton Park**
- **Address:** 438 Cooke St, Waterbury, CT 06710
- **Type:** Outdoor
- **What's there:** **70-acre Olmsted Brothers–designed park** with separate playgrounds for small and big kids, a seasonal splash pad, fishing ponds, fountains, walking paths. Good for a 30–45 minute energy burn.
- **Detour:** ~5 minutes from I-84
- **Cost:** Free
- **Hours:** Dawn to dusk
- **Bathrooms:** Yes
- **Fenced:** Playground area is somewhat enclosed

**INDOOR: ProKids Place (inside Brass Mill Center mall)**
- **Address:** 495 Union St #2086, Waterbury, CT 06706
- **Type:** Indoor
- **What's there:** Soft indoor playground for kids under 10 — slides, nets, tunnels, ninja course, large ball pit, interactive game wall. Mall location means food court and restrooms steps away.
- **Detour:** Right off I-84, Exit 21/22
- **Cost:** ~$10–15/child
- **Hours:** Mon–Sat 11 AM–8 PM, Sun 11 AM–6 PM
- **Phone:** (203) 841-8249
- **Bathrooms:** Yes (mall restrooms)

### Interval 3 — Poughkeepsie / Kingston NY (~90 minutes from Waterbury)

**INDOOR: Mid-Hudson Children's Museum — Poughkeepsie**
- **Address:** 75 N Water St, Poughkeepsie, NY 12601
- **Type:** Indoor
- **What's there:** Award-winning children's museum across two floors — Early Learning Junction with a play village (train station, fire truck, restaurant), Imagination Playground, mastodon fossil climbing area. Voted #2 "Best Place to Take Kids" in the Hudson Valley. A 4-year-old could easily do 30–45 focused minutes here.
- **Detour:** ~5 minutes from I-84
- **Cost:** **$14.50/person** (under 1 free). Free parking on-site.
- **Hours:** Wed–Sat 9:30 AM–5 PM, Sun 11 AM–5 PM (closed Mon)
- **Phone:** (845) 471-0589
- **Bathrooms:** Yes

**OUTDOOR: Forsyth Park and Nature Center — Kingston**
- **Address:** 157 Lucas Ave, Kingston, NY 12401
- **Type:** Outdoor
- **What's there:** Playground with slides and multi-structure equipment, plus the **free Forsyth Nature Center** with 24+ animal exhibits and gardens. Perfect final stretch stop — kids love seeing the animals. 20–30 minutes is ideal.
- **Detour:** ~10 minutes from NY-28/I-87
- **Cost:** Free
- **Hours:** Park daily; Nature Center typically 9 AM–4 PM
- **Bathrooms:** Yes

**OUTDOOR ALTERNATE: Kingston Point Park**
- **Address:** Delaware Ave, Kingston, NY 12401 (end of Broadway, waterfront)
- **Type:** Outdoor
- **What's there:** Brand-new playground with Hudson River views, walking trails, pavilions. The adjacent **Catskill Mountain Railroad** runs seasonal heritage train rides (check CMRRevents.com).
- **Cost:** Free
- **Bathrooms:** Yes

Scenic lake with ducks and swans to feed. Walking paths, shade trees. The free Washington County Museum of Fine Arts is inside the park if weather turns.

### Interval 3 — the hardest stretch: Vicksburg MS to Monroe LA (~85 miles of nothing)

This is the most barren stretch on the entire trip. **There are almost no kid-friendly facilities between Vicksburg and Monroe.** Here are the limited options:

**OUTDOOR (DETOUR): Poverty Point World Heritage Site — near Delhi, LA**
- **Address:** I-20 Exit 153 at Delhi, then ~25 miles north via Hwy 17 → Hwy 134 → Hwy 577
- **Type:** Outdoor
- **What's there:** **UNESCO World Heritage Site** with 3,400-year-old earthen mounds. Tram tours at 10 AM, 11:30 AM, 1 PM, 3 PM. Kids can climb the massive 72-foot Bird Mound. Interactive museum with hands-on displays. Picnic areas.
- **Cost:** $4/adult; **free for children 12 and under**
- **Hours:** Daily 9 AM–5 PM
- **⚠️ Detour warning:** This is 25 miles off I-20 — a significant detour (50 miles round trip, ~45 minutes). Only worthwhile if you need a major stop in this dead zone.
- **Bathrooms:** Yes

**BACKUP OPTION: Rest areas on I-20 in Louisiana** — Louisiana's I-20 rest areas are basic with limited or no play equipment. They do have restrooms, picnic tables, and sometimes grassy areas where a child can run. Check for the rest area near **Tallulah (mile marker ~171)** and **Delhi area**.

**PRACTICAL REALITY:** For this 85-mile stretch, your best bet is to time the drive so the child naps, or plan a **quick grass-running stop at any rest area** along the way.

## Quick-reference picks by situation

**When you only have 15 minutes** — Withers Park (Wytheville VA), Veterans Memorial Park (Abingdon VA), and any Fairfield TX or Brookhaven MS city park work for a fast playground blitz right off the highway.

**When weather turns bad** — The Connecticut Science Center, Mid-Hudson Children's Museum, Discovery Station (Hagerstown), Kids Square (Roanoke), Mississippi Children's Museum (Meridian), and Kanga's Indoor Playcenter (Oak Ridge North TX) are all purpose-built for indoor energy burn in under 45 minutes.

**Best fenced playgrounds** — Optimist Park (Hattiesburg MS) has a **gated playground with a single entry/exit**, the ideal setup for managing a 4-year-old near a parking lot. Chambersburg Memorial Park's tot playground is also partially enclosed.

**Best splash pads for late April** — Pack Square/Splasheville (Asheville NC) is the premier option if it's warm enough. Bear Branch Sprayground (The Woodlands TX) typically opens in March and should be running. Southern splash pads in Mississippi and Alabama generally don't open until Memorial Day.

**The Louisiana dead zone strategy** — Between Vicksburg and Monroe (~85 miles), there is essentially nothing. Time this stretch for a nap, fuel up in Vicksburg, and plan Monroe's Louisiana Purchase Gardens & Zoo as your reward stop. If the child needs to move, pull into any rest area for a 10-minute grass run.

**Star stops worth building the day around** — Gypsy Hill Park (Staunton VA), Noccalula Falls Park (Gadsden AL), Mississippi Children's Museum (Meridian), Louisiana Purchase Gardens & Zoo (Monroe LA), and Bear Branch Park (The Woodlands TX) are all exceptional enough to justify 45–90 minutes and will leave everyone — including the 4-year-old — genuinely happy.
