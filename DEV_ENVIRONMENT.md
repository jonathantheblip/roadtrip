# Dev environment reality

This document is project-level and load-bearing. It is not a
working file. It does not get edited without explicit instruction
from Jonathan. Every carryover references this file by path AND
inlines the full text near the top.

## The user

Helen is the user. Specifically: she wants exactly what she wants,
when she wants it, is not always patient, and doesn't fully grasp
that her requests are complex to meet at her quality bar. That is
the environment. Not "average user." Not "MVP." Helen.

## Implications that bind

1. Mid-trip rush mode is a real state. When Jonathan signals he's
   in one, priority features ship as soon as it's safe — meaning
   the gate passes, not "feels good." This may force hard choices
   between competing requests. Make the tradeoff explicit, name
   what gets deprioritized, move.

2. Cadence interruptions from Helen requests are the norm, not
   the exception. The roadmap survives them by being short enough
   to re-sequence cleanly, not by being defended.

3. Quality-bar-at-Helen is the standard. Shipping fast does not
   mean shipping degraded. The bug-trap exists so fast and good
   are not in tension. Use it.

4. No MVP framing. "Minimum viable" maps to a user who tolerates
   friction in exchange for early access. Helen is not that user.
   The minimum is the bar.

5. Time estimates from Claude are unreliable and have cost real
   plans. Don't give them. Scope is small / medium / large; the
   calendar is Jonathan's.
